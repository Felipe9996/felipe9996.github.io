Offroading and Overlanding in the Tatacoa Desert

❮ ❯

The Tatacoa Desert is the 2nd largest arid zone in Colombia after the Guajira Peninsula, and offers breathtaking night skies for those feeling particularly adventurous and wanting to stay a few nights in the middle of this desert. As some of you already know, I've been to the Tatacoa desert and know a few things about the trails there. Without further ado, I present to you, my list of things to consider when travelling to the Tatacoa desert:

1) Your car must be up to the task

If you want the full experience of this beautiful desert, you must navigate deep inside its borders, far from society and with no chance of getting help should you get stuck. If you're travelling with a group, such as Colombia Offroad or similar 4x4 communities, you can get away with a simple 4wd SUV, as the others will help you get through any obstacles beyond your vehicle's capabilities. If you're alone, however, you must have a capable 4x4 vehicle, and in both scenarios your car must be in good shape, as any issues you can't fix on the road are effectively unfixable here. There's no roadside assistance for the offroaders and overlanders in Colombia, so you must be your own mechanic.

2) Bring enough supplies

When you're deep in the desert, you're on your own. There are no gas stations, restaurants, or any semblance of society, so you must bring everything you'll need with you. Fill up your car's tank before entering the desert, and if your car isn't very fuel efficient, it's best to bring a jerry can full of extra fuel just in case. You should also pack enough food for your stay and plenty of water, and a toolbox with the required tools for any troubleshooting and fixing you may have to do on your car should you be unlucky enough to break down.

3) Know your way through

When leaving the more known tourist areas and heading deep inside this beautiful desert, it's easy to get lost, so you should bring a map of the trails with you. Digital maps such as GaiaGPS are great options to navigate the desert, and they even allow you to mark your route beforehand or even download someone else's published route, but you should still have a paper map with you as a backup. Getting lost deep inside the desert is very dangerous, so always keep an eye on your location. If you're travelling with a group specialized in 4x4 trips such as Colombia Offroad, this should be a non-issue, but keep your group's route handy just in case you accidentally get left behind.

4) Everything you bring with you returns with you

I'd think this should be obvious enough, but as some people have proven, it has to be specified. Be respectful to nature and don't leave trash behind in the desert, as it contaminates this beautiful and mostly untouched part of Colombia, and slowly kills what little fauna and flora inhabits this beautiful desert.

5) Sleeping in the desert

If you really want to experience the true beauty of this desert, you should ignore the hotels stationed in the more well known areas of the desert and just sleep deep in the desert, with the stars above you and peaceful silence around you. A normal tent can do the trick, but a roof mounted tent is a great option if your car's compatible with one, as you don't have to deal with fixating it to the sand. You can also bring a sleeping bag and fall asleep under the beautiful night sky instead, if you like sleeping in the open.

By following this 5 tips, I can guarantee you'll have a safe and enjoyable experience in this beautiful gem of Colombia.
